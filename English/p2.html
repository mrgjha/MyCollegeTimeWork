The Simple Past



    

  1. The Simple Past Tense can also be used 
for habitual actions and states in the past.

When it is used in this way it usually has 
the same adverbs as the simple present tense:




Last year I walked the office every day.
=Last year I used to walk to the office every day.

My drandmother always went to Switzerland 
for her holidays.
He got angry every time I asked for money.
He always carried an umbrella.
They never drank wine.





  2. It is used for actions completed in the 
past at a definite time.


I met him yesterday
Pasteur died in 1985.
(time implied)


when did you meet him ?
(time is asked about)

The train was ten minutes late.
how did you get your present job.
I bought this car in montreal.

(The action clearly took place 
at a definite time 
even though this time is not mentioned)





  3. where have you been ?-
I have been to the opera.
did you enjoy it?

(sometimes the time becomes definite as a result of 
quest. and ans. in the present perfect)



  4. Occured at a moment 
in period of time now terminated

My grandmother once saw Queen Victoria.
Did you ever hear maria calls sing?





  5. Time is not given but which 
occupied a period of time now terminated


He worked in that bank for four years
(but he doesn't work there now)

She lived in Rome for a long time
(but she is not living there now)





  6. I wish,If Only



I wish I were a prince.(I wish to be a prince)  
I wish I were rich.(I wish to be rich)


I wish I knew him.(I don't know him)
I wish I had known him.(I didn't know him)

If only I knew him(I don't know him)
If only I had known him(I didn't know him)


It is time we started(it is time for us to start)
It is time you finished(It is time for you to finish)


  7. Use a past tense to ask when ? or what time ?

when did they arrive ? (not 'have they arrived')
what time did you finish work ?
